Access hidden WooCommerce options such as: Disable Confirm Logout, Hide Password Strength Meter, Hide Categories from Shop Pages and Widgets, Show Empty Product Categories, Hide Product Category Name/Count, Sort Shipping Methods from Least Expensive to Most Expensive, Hide Related Products on product page, Force Local Pickup Shipping Method to be last choice, Disable New User Registration from wp-login.php screen.

== Description ==

Easy Options for WooCommerce will allow you to access some hidden options that are not normally available to you.

* CONFIRM LOGOUT: Allows you to disable to the Confirm Logout question on the WooCommerce My-Account page.

* SHIPPING: Allows you to sort the shipping methods from least expensive to most expensive. Allows you to force the Local Pickup shipping method to be at the bottom as the last choice.

* PASSWORD STRENGTH METER: Allows you to hide or show the Password Strength Meter when new users are creating their password.

* HIDE RELATED PRODUCTS: Allows you to hide the Related Products section on single product pages.

* EMPTY PRODUCT CATEGORIES: Let's you choose to show or hide empty product categories on the Shop pages.

* HIDE CATEGORIES: Choose categories to hide from shop pages and widgets.

* PRODUCT CATEGORY TEXT: Let's you choose to show or hide the title/text that appears under each product category image on the Shop pages.

* PRODUCT CATEGORY COUNT: Let's you choose to show or hide the count of items that appears under each product category image on the Shop pages.

* DISABLE NEW USER REGISTRATION: If you are using the WooCommerce registration pages on My-Account page and/or during checkout, you can choose to disable the new user registration form that appears on wp-login.php?action=register . This can help reduce spam bot sign ups.

* Tested with WooCommerce v3.2.3+   It may work with older versions of WooCommerce, but no guarantees.

== Frequently Asked Questions ==

= Empty Product Categories are not showing up =

* You may need to change Shop Page Display to include Categories.
* You may need to change Default Category Display to include Subcategories.
* You can find both these settings on Appearance > Customize > WooCommerce > Product Catalog.
